## General Notes

**Climactic Structure:** This chunk is the emotional climax - Irma dies, Elza revealed as chimera, protagonist revealed as chimera (RED test), but his consciousness is still human.

**The Hedgehog Riddle:** Brilliant test - real Elza would answer "porcupine" and explain why (one big quill vs many small ones). Chimera-Elza says "hedgehog is hedgehog" and can't explain "why" (causality blindness).

**Shapeshifting Horror:** The white fungal substance can create perfect visual disguises - made Alex look like Nathan Gog, complete with facial features. Only tested by physical details (missing tattoo).

**Red Test Result:** Protagonist IS chimera by biological test, but his consciousness remained in control (established: his genetic disorder prevented full transformation). He's unique.

**Alex's Soul Question:** Pivotal moment - if souls exist but science can't detect them, then maybe original Irma and Elza's souls are still "in there" trapped inside the chimera bodies. This could be important for the ending.

**Combat Effectiveness:** Despite being chimeras, they can be killed - Irma died from Shiva rifle, white substance burns/dies. They're powerful but not invincible.

**Protagonist's Moral Crisis:** He killed Irma (woman he loved), discovered his daughter is replaced by a monster, and learned he himself is technically a chimera. Maximum psychological damage.

**"Black Sleeves" Location:** Unclear what this refers to - possibly a building, area of colony, or equipment storage. Context suggests a secure area where testing happens.
