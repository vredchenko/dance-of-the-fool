## General Notes

**Transmarginal Inhibition:** This is a real Pavlovian concept - when an organism is subjected to overwhelming stress, the nervous system protects itself by shutting down. This will be important for defeating chimeras.

**Hand Regeneration:** The most graphic body horror scene yet. The detailed description of fungal growth mimicking human tissue is both fascinating and horrifying. The protagonist is now undeniably part of the mycelium.

**Consciousness Duality:** Major philosophical question - if the mycelium perfectly copies a person including memories and personality, but the original consciousness is suppressed, is this death or transformation? The protagonist is unique because he remained "at the wheel."

**The Real Irma:** Strong suggestion that original Irma's consciousness still exists, trapped inside the chimera body, experiencing everything but unable to control anything. This makes Irma/chimera's actions even more tragic.

**Moral Complexity:** Irma/mycelium genuinely believes in the symbiosis vision. From their perspective, offering humanity eternal life and regeneration in exchange for technology is fair trade. They don't understand they're asking for species extinction.

**Name Blindness:** Royal chimeras can't remember certain names - possibly names with strong emotional/personal significance. They can repeat them but not recall them later. This is their one reliable "tell."

**Causality Blindness:** The mycelium thinks in webs of simultaneous causes rather than linear cause-and-effect. This makes "why" questions incomprehensible to them - there's no single "why," only multiple contributing factors.

**Spitting Attack:** Irma's ability to spit fungal material as a weapon suggests chimeras can weaponize their fungal components. This might be how they infect/transform others rapidly.
